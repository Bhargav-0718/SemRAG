He believed that true social justice could not be achieved without the abolition of caste, which he viewed as a fundamental barrier to equality and dignity for all individuals.

In his seminal work, "Annihilation of Caste," Ambedkar emphasized the need for reform within the Hindu social structure. He argued that the caste system, which categorizes individuals based on hereditary occupation and social status, is inherently unjust. He contended that the first step towards reform should be the abolition of sub-castes, as he believed that mere changes within the existing caste framework would not lead to genuine equality. He pointed out the complexities of caste hierarchies, explaining how, for instance, Brahmins from different regions could occupy varying social standings, which undermines the supposed uniformity that caste advocates claim.

Ambedkar was particularly critical of the Hindu scriptures, which he argued support the existing caste system and untouchability. He asserted that if the Shastras, which include texts like the Vedas and Smritis, endorse these social injustices, then he could no longer identify as a Hindu. He famously stated, “If Caste and Varna are convertible terms and if Varna is an integral part of the Shastras which define Hinduism, I do not know how a person who rejects Caste can call himself a Hindu.” This reflects his belief that religious identity cannot be separated from social justice issues, as he saw the structures of caste as fundamentally opposed to the principles of equality.

Furthermore, Ambedkar proposed that any religion that supports caste and untouchability cannot be justified, and he voiced that one should abandon such beliefs in pursuit of justice. His stance was that social justice is not merely about reforming the existing system but requires a radical transformation of society to eliminate all forms of discrimination and inequality.

In summary, Dr. B.R. Ambedkar's views on social justice were characterized by a strong denunciation of the caste system, a call for its abolition, and a plea for a reexamination of religious texts that perpetuate social hierarchies. His advocacy for equality and dignity for all individuals remains a cornerstone of his legacy in the fight for social justice in India.
